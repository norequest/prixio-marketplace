# Contract Guidance for IT Companies in Georgia

> **Sources**: Civil Code of Georgia, Law on Electronic Commerce (2024),
> Law on Consumer Rights Protection, Georgian legal practice.
> **Last updated**: March 2026.

## Governing Legal Framework

| Area | Primary Law |
|------|------------|
| General contracts | Civil Code of Georgia |
| Employment contracts | Labor Code of Georgia |
| Consumer protection | Law on Consumer Rights Protection |
| E-commerce | Law on Electronic Commerce (2024) |
| Data protection | Law on Personal Data Protection (2023) |
| IP assignment | Law on Copyright and Neighboring Rights (1999) |

## 1. Software Development Agreement (SDA)

### Essential Clauses

1. **Scope of Work**
   - Detailed specifications or reference to separate SOW document
   - Acceptance criteria for deliverables
   - Change request procedure

2. **Timeline and Milestones**
   - Development phases with deadlines
   - Milestone delivery dates
   - Penalty for delays (if applicable)

3. **Payment Terms**
   - Fixed price, time & materials, or milestone-based
   - Payment schedule tied to milestones
   - Currency (GEL or USD/EUR)
   - Late payment interest

4. **Intellectual Property**
   - IP ownership transfer upon payment (recommended for clients)
   - Or: IP license granted (recommended for platform companies)
   - Pre-existing IP exclusions
   - Open source components disclosure

5. **Warranty**
   - Bug-fix period (typically 3-6 months after delivery)
   - Definition of "defect" vs "feature request"
   - Response time SLAs

6. **Confidentiality**
   - Mutual NDA terms
   - Duration: typically 2-5 years after contract end
   - Exceptions: public knowledge, independent development, legal requirement

7. **Liability**
   - Cap on total liability (typically 100-200% of contract value)
   - Exclusion of indirect/consequential damages
   - Insurance requirements (if applicable)

8. **Termination**
   - Termination for convenience (with notice period)
   - Termination for cause (material breach + cure period)
   - IP and payment consequences on termination

9. **Dispute Resolution**
   - Governing law: Georgian law (or mutually agreed)
   - Arbitration: Georgian International Arbitration Centre or GIAC
   - Alternative: Tbilisi City Court

### Georgian Legal Requirements
- Contract language: **Georgian** required for court enforcement (include English translation)
- Stamp duty: **None** for standard commercial contracts
- Notarization: **Not required** (but recommended for high-value contracts)
- Electronic contracts: **Valid** under Law on Electronic Commerce

## 2. SaaS Terms of Service

### Required Under Georgian Law (E-Commerce + Data Protection)

1. **Service Description**
   - What the SaaS provides
   - Service levels (uptime guarantees)
   - Feature limitations

2. **Account Registration**
   - Age requirements (if applicable)
   - Account responsibility
   - Multi-user provisions

3. **Subscription and Payment**
   - Pricing and billing cycle
   - Auto-renewal terms (must be clearly disclosed)
   - Refund policy
   - Price change notification (reasonable advance notice required)

4. **User Content**
   - User retains ownership of uploaded content
   - License granted to provider for service operation
   - Content moderation policies
   - Prohibited content

5. **Data Protection**
   - Link to Privacy Policy
   - Data processing purposes
   - Sub-processors disclosure
   - Data portability on account closure

6. **Intellectual Property**
   - Platform IP belongs to provider
   - User content IP belongs to user
   - Feedback and suggestions license

7. **Limitation of Liability**
   - Cap on liability (GEL amount or subscription fees paid)
   - Force majeure clause
   - Service availability disclaimers

8. **Termination**
   - User can terminate anytime
   - Provider termination with notice
   - Data export period after termination (minimum 30 days recommended)

### E-Commerce Law (2024) Requirements
- Clear identification of the service provider (name, address, registration number)
- Full price transparency (including all taxes and fees)
- **Right of withdrawal**: 14 days for consumers (can be waived for digital content with consent)
- Electronic contract formation rules
- Pre-contractual information duties
- **Enforcement**: National Competition Agency of Georgia (gcca.gov.ge)
- **Penalties**: Up to **2% of previous year's annual turnover**; doubles for repeat violations within 12 months
- Must provide information in **Georgian language** and consumer's language in clear, understandable manner
- Defines obligations for **trading platform intermediaries**

## 3. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

### Key Terms

| Element | Recommended Provision |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Parties** | Full legal names and registration numbers |
| **Definition of Confidential Info** | Broad but specific (include: code, algorithms, business data, customer lists) |
| **Exclusions** | Public knowledge, prior knowledge, independent development, legal requirement |
| **Obligations** | Don't disclose, use only for intended purpose, reasonable security measures |
| **Duration** | 2-5 years (trade secrets: indefinite until public) |
| **Return of materials** | Return/destroy all confidential materials on request |
| **Remedies** | Injunctive relief + damages |
| **Governing law** | Georgian law |

### Types of NDAs
1. **Mutual NDA** — both parties share confidential info (most common for partnerships)
2. **One-way NDA** — one party shares (employee, investor presentations)
3. **Multi-party NDA** — three or more parties (consortium projects)

### Enforceability in Georgia
- NDAs are **fully enforceable** under Georgian contract law
- Georgian courts will grant **injunctions** to prevent disclosure
- Damages can include: actual damages + lost profits + unjust enrichment
- **Criminal liability** possible for trade secret theft

## 4. Employment Contract for Developers

### Required Clauses (Labor Code + IT Best Practices)

1. **Standard employment terms** (see Employment Law skill for full requirements)

2. **IP Assignment Clause** (critical for IT):
   ```
   All intellectual property created by the Employee during the course of
   employment, including but not limited to source code, algorithms, designs,
   documentation, and inventions, shall be the exclusive property of the Employer.
   The Employee hereby assigns all rights, title, and interest in such works
   to the Employer.
   ```

3. **Confidentiality Clause**:
   - Survives termination (2-5 years recommended)
   - Covers: source code, customer data, business strategies, financial info

4. **Non-Compete Clause** (optional):
   - Duration: max 12 months recommended
   - Scope: specific competing products/services
   - Compensation: 30-50% of salary during restriction period

5. **Equipment and Tools**:
   - Company-provided hardware/software
   - BYOD policy (if applicable)
   - Return of equipment on termination

6. **Remote Work Terms** (if applicable):
   - Work location flexibility
   - Core hours requirements
   - Communication expectations
   - Data security requirements

## 5. Freelancer/Contractor Agreement

### Essential Differences from Employment

| Clause | Employment Contract | Contractor Agreement |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Relationship | Employer-Employee | Client-Service Provider |
| Tax | Employer withholds | Contractor pays own taxes |
| IP | Work-for-hire (automatic) | Must be explicitly assigned |
| Schedule | Set by employer | Flexible |
| Liability | Employer liable | Contractor liable for deliverables |
| Termination | Labor Code protections | Per contract terms |

### Must-Include Clauses
1. **Independent contractor status** — explicitly state no employment relationship
2. **IP assignment** — must be explicit (work-for-hire does NOT automatically apply)
3. **Deliverables** — specific, measurable outputs
4. **Payment** — milestones, hourly, or project-based
5. **Tax responsibility** — contractor responsible for own taxes
6. **Insurance** — contractor's own liability coverage
7. **Subcontracting** — whether allowed

## 7. Investment Agreements for Startups

### Common Structures in Georgia

1. **SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)**
   - Popular for early-stage funding
   - Not specifically regulated in Georgian law
   - Enforceable as general civil contract
   - Must comply with Civil Code

2. **Convertible Note**
   - Debt instrument converting to equity
   - Interest and maturity date provisions
   - Conversion discount and valuation cap

3. **Share Purchase Agreement (SPA)**
   - Direct equity sale
   - Governed by Law on Entrepreneurs
   - Requires charter amendment for new shares
   - Registration at NAPR (free of charge)

4. **Shareholders' Agreement**
   - Not required by law but strongly recommended
   - Governs: voting, drag/tag-along, pre-emptive rights, exit provisions
   - Enforceable under Civil Code
   - Can include: vesting, good/bad leaver, anti-dilution

### Key Considerations
- Georgia has **no securities commission** (no startup fundraising registration)
- No **crowdfunding regulation** (legal gray area)
- **Foreign investment** fully protected by constitution and bilateral treaties
- **Arbitration** recommended for international investment disputes

## 8. Dispute Resolution Options

| Method | Cost | Speed | Binding? | Best For |
|--------|------|-------|----------|----------|
| **Negotiation** | Free | Fast | No | Most disputes first |
| **Mediation** | Low | 1-3 months | No (unless agreed) | Relationship preservation |
| **Arbitration (GIAC)** | Medium | 3-9 months | Yes | International disputes |
| **Court litigation** | Medium | 6-18 months | Yes | Domestic enforcement |

### Georgian International Arbitration Centre (GIAC)
- Modern arbitration institution
- English-language proceedings available
- Enforceable internationally (Georgia is NY Convention member)
- Preferred by international investors and tech companies

## 9. Electronic Contract Rules (2024 E-Commerce Law)

### Valid Electronic Contracts
- Electronic contracts have **same legal force** as paper contracts
- **Electronic signatures** are legally binding
- **Click-wrap** and **browse-wrap** agreements are enforceable
- Must provide clear pre-contractual information

### Required Disclosures for Online Services
1. Service provider identity (full legal name, address, ID number)
2. Full price including taxes
3. Payment methods accepted
4. Delivery terms (if applicable)
5. Right of withdrawal information
6. Complaint handling procedure
7. Contact information
