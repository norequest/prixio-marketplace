## 1. Virtual Zone Person (VZP) Status

**Legal Basis**: Law on Information Technology Zones (2010), Tax Code Art. 8, 99¹

### What It Is
A VZP is a legal person engaged in information technology activities holding a certificate
issued by LEPL Financial-Analytical Service of the Ministry of Finance of Georgia.

### Tax Benefits
- **0% Corporate Income Tax** on profits from IT services/products exported outside Georgia
- **0% VAT** on IT services supplied to non-Georgian clients
- Dividends still subject to **5% withholding tax** (unless reduced by DTA)
- Still subject to all other standard taxes (salary, pension, property)

### Eligibility Requirements
1. Must be a Georgian-registered **legal entity** (LLC/JSC)
2. Must engage in **IT activities**: software development, design, production, implementation
3. Must **develop software within Georgia** (local presence required)
4. Must deliver IT services/products to **clients located outside Georgia**
5. Must maintain **technical staff** in Georgia

### Application Process
1. Register company at House of Justice / justice.gov.ge
2. Apply for VZP status at **LEPL Financial-Analytical Service** (online)
3. Submit: company registration docs, description of IT activities, client contracts
4. Processing time: **10 working days**
5. Receive digital VZP certificate (shared with Revenue Service automatically)
6. Certificate is **publicly available** on agency website

### Status Validity
- Valid **indefinitely** (no renewal needed)
- Can be revoked if: company applies to waive it, or tax authorities suspect tax evasion
- Must file **monthly corporate declarations** with Revenue Service

### What VZP Does NOT Cover
- Income from Georgian domestic clients (taxed at standard 15% CIT)
- Employee salary taxes (20% PIT + 4% pension still apply)
- VAT on domestic supplies (18% if VAT-registered)
- Property tax (up to 1%)
- **5% withholding tax** on royalties paid to non-residents (unless DTA exempt)
- **18% reverse VAT** on services received from non-resident companies/individuals
- **10% withholding tax** on service fees paid to non-residents

### Compliance Requirements
- Maintain **detailed records** (contracts, invoices, bank statements)
- Be prepared for **audits** by Financial-Analytical Service
- Demonstrate that software is developed **on Georgian territory**
- Revenue Service **methodical instruction** (Dec 2022) tightened interpretation

## 2. International Company (IC) Status

**Legal Basis**: Tax Code of Georgia, Art. 23¹, 98¹

### Tax Benefits
- **5% CIT** (instead of 15%) on distributed profits; reinvested profits remain untaxed
- **5% PIT** (instead of 20%) on employee salaries
- **0% dividend withholding** for non-resident shareholders
- **5% dividend withholding** for resident shareholders
- **0% property tax** on non-land property used for permitted activities

### Eligible IT Activities (with NACE Codes)
| NACE Code | Activity |
|-----------|----------|
| **58.2** | Software publishing (incl. computer game development) |
| **62.01** | Computer programming activities |
| **62.02** | Information technology consultancy |
| **62.03** | Computer facilities management |
| **62.09** | Other IT and computer service activities |
| — | Digital product creation and delivery |
| — | Website development and hosting |
| — | Remote system administration |
| — | Software maintenance and updates |
| — | Database access provision |
| — | SaaS software (sales force, accounting, antivirus, etc.) |
| — | Automatic online security (firewalls, filters) |

### Eligibility Requirements
1. Must be a Georgian-registered entity (LLC or JSC)
2. At least **98% of revenue** must come from IT services exported outside Georgia
3. Must not derive more than **2%** from Georgian domestic clients
4. Must demonstrate **2+ years operational experience** in qualifying IT sectors
5. Must perform **key value-generating activities** from within Georgia
6. Must have **adequate human resources** with necessary qualifications in Georgia
7. Cannot operate within Free Industrial Zones

### Experience Criteria (one of three paths)
1. Company has 2+ years experience in the permitted activity
2. Parent company (50%+ ownership) has 2+ years experience in the activity
3. Georgian subsidiary representing foreign enterprise with 2+ years experience

### Deductible Expenses (can reduce effective rate below 5%)
- Salaries paid to **Georgian citizen resident employees**
- **Scientific research** costs directly connected to permitted activities
- **Design and experimental-construction** services
- **R&D expenditures** and prototype development

### Application Process
1. Submit application on **rs.ge** portal with:
   - Enterprise registration details and ID numbers
   - Legal and operational addresses
   - Permitted activity classifications
   - Service delivery location (Georgia)
2. Revenue Service reviews within **10 business days**
3. Ministry of Finance grants final approval
4. Status effective the **first day of the approval month**
5. Status valid **indefinitely** (subject to continued compliance)
6. Status revoked with 10 working days notice if >2% revenue from non-permitted activities

### VAT Considerations
- Unlike VZP, broad VAT exemptions do NOT automatically apply
- B2B services rendered outside Georgia typically qualify for VAT exemptions
- VAT-registered IC companies may recover input VAT on purchases

### IC vs VZP Comparison
| Factor | VZP | IC Status |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| CIT on exports | 0% | 5% |
| PIT on salaries | 20% | 5% |
| Dividend (non-resident) | 5% | 0% |
| Domestic revenue allowed | Yes (taxed normally) | Max 2% |
| Eligible activities | Narrow (software dev only) | Broader IT categories |
| Experience required | No | 2+ years |
| Legal clarity | Lower (methodical instruction) | Higher (NACE codes in law) |
| Status holder | Legal entity only | Legal entity only |
| Best for | Pure export IT (profit focus) | Companies with employees (payroll focus) |

**Key insight**: IC Status is better if you have many employees (saves 15% on PIT per employee). VZP is better if you mainly want to shelter corporate profits. IC provides more legal clarity on eligible activities.

## 3. GITA Innovative Startup Status

**Legal Basis**: Government Resolution (2025), Tax Code amendments

### Tax Benefits (up to 10 years)
| Years | PIT Rate | CIT Rate | Withholding Tax |
|-------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| 1-3 | 0% (salary up to 10,000 GEL/mo) | 0% (exempt) | 0% |
| 4-6 | 5% (salary up to 10,000 GEL/mo) | 5% | 5% |
| 7-10 | 10% (salary up to 10,000 GEL/mo) | 10% | 10% |

### Additional Benefits
- **300% tax credit** for innovative SMEs on qualified R&D expenditures
- **30% cashback** on R&D expenses
- Access to GITA **grant programs** ($2,000–$250,000)
- Access to GITA **technology parks** (9 nationwide, including Tbilisi)

### GITA Grant Programs
| Program | Amount | Stage |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Idea prototyping | $2,000–$6,000 | Pre-seed |
| Pre-seed grants | $35,000 | Early stage |
| Seed funding | Up to $250,000 | Growth |
| Matching Grants | Case by case | 279 startups funded, $15.9M total, attracted $217M private capital |

### Eligibility
- Must demonstrate **innovation** (new/significantly improved product, process, or service)
- Must be approved by **GITA** (gita.gov.ge) — reviewed within **15 business days**
- Cannot hold VZP or IC status simultaneously (obtaining either revokes startup status)
- Must be a Georgian-registered entity (LLC or JSC)
- No tax debts, not in debtors' registry, no insolvency proceedings
- Must have received within prior 2 years:
  - Min **100,000 GEL** investment from registered investment funds/angel networks/accelerators, OR
  - Min **150,000 GEL** grant from GITA
- Registration fee: **200 GEL**

### Growth Stage Investment Requirements
| Period | Minimum Total Investment | From Approved Sources |
|--------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Years 3-5 renewal | 5,000,000 GEL | At least 30% |
| Years 6-10 renewal | 15,000,000 GEL | At least 30% |

### Complementary R&D Regimes (cannot be combined with Startup Status)
| Regime | Eligibility | Benefits | Fee |
|--------|------------|----------|-----|
| **Innovative SME** | R&D expenses ≥5% of annual revenue (min 100K GEL) | 3x R&D deduction + 20% reinvestment grant (max 100K GEL) | 500 GEL |
| **R&D Service Provider** | ≥80% revenue from NACE 72.1 | 5% PIT + 5% CIT (permanent) | 500 GEL |

## 4. Small Business Status (Individual Entrepreneur)

**Legal Basis**: Tax Code Art. 88–90

### Tax Benefits
- **1% turnover tax** (instead of 20% PIT) on income up to **500,000 GEL/year**
- **3%** on amount exceeding 500,000 GEL threshold
- For **agritourism**: threshold is 700,000 GEL

### Eligibility
- Must be an **Individual Entrepreneur** (IE), not LLC
- Must not be in restricted business activities (banking, gambling, financial services)
- No Georgian citizenship or residency requirement
- Can have employees (salary withholding still applies)

### Compliance
- **Monthly** income tax declarations on rs.ge (even if zero income)
- Status auto-revoked January 1 of third year if threshold exceeded for 2 consecutive years
- Not compatible with LLC structure

## 5. Free Industrial Zone (FIZ) Enterprise

**Legal Basis**: Law on Free Industrial Zones

### Tax Benefits
- **0% CIT** on profits
- **0% VAT** on supplies within FIZ
- **0% customs duties** on imports
- **0% property tax**
- **0% dividend tax**

### Georgia's FIZs
- Kutaisi Free Industrial Zone
- Poti Free Industrial Zone
- Tbilisi Free Industrial Zone (planned)

### Eligibility
- Must have **physical presence** within FIZ
- No special licenses required for IT companies
- Best for hardware/manufacturing, less relevant for pure software
