# Employment Law for IT Companies in Georgia

> **Sources**: matsne.gov.ge (Labor Code), Ministry of IDPs, Labour, Health and
> Social Affairs, Fragomen immigration guides, KPMG Georgia.
> **Last updated**: March 2026.

## Legal Framework

**Labor Code of Georgia** (შრომის კოდექსი)
- Original: 2006
- Major amendments: 2020 (EU association agreement alignment)
- Governs: employment relationships, contracts, working conditions, termination

---

## 1. Employment Contracts

### Required Elements (Art. 14)
1. **Parties' identities** — employer and employee full details
2. **Job description** — position, duties, responsibilities
3. **Start date** and duration (fixed-term or indefinite)
4. **Working hours** — standard: 40 hours/week (Art. 14)
5. **Remuneration** — salary amount, payment schedule, bonuses
6. **Leave entitlement** — annual, sick, maternity/paternity
7. **Notice period** — for termination (minimum 30 days)
8. **Work location** — office, remote, or hybrid

### Contract Types
| Type | Duration | Use Case |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Indefinite** | No end date | Permanent employees |
| **Fixed-term** | Up to 30 months | Project-based, seasonal |
| **Part-time** | Reduced hours | Flexible arrangements |

### Key Provisions for IT Companies
- Contract must be in **writing** (oral contracts are valid but hard to enforce)
- Must be in **Georgian** (can include English translation)
- **IP assignment clause** — essential for developers (see IP Protection skill)
- **Confidentiality clause** — standard for tech employees
- **Non-compete clause** — enforceable with limitations

## 2. Probation Period (გამოსაცდელი ვადა)

| Aspect | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Maximum duration | **6 months** |
| Minimum salary | No requirement during probation |
| Termination during probation | **3 days written notice** (no reason needed) |
| Extension | Cannot be extended beyond 6 months |
| Repeat probation | Not allowed for same position |

## 3. Working Hours and Overtime

| Category | Limit |
|----------|-------|
| Standard work week | **40 hours** |
| Daily maximum | **8 hours** (can be extended to 12 with weekly limit) |
| Overtime rate | **125%** of regular pay (for first 2 hours), **150%** thereafter |
| Night work (22:00-06:00) | **+20%** premium |
| Weekly rest | Minimum **24 consecutive hours** |
| Hazardous work | **36 hours/week** maximum |

## 5. Remote Work (დისტანციური მუშაობა)

### Legal Framework
- Labor Code Art. 14 allows remote work arrangements
- Must be specified in employment contract or addendum
- Employer must ensure occupational safety for remote workers

### Best Practices for IT Companies
1. **Written agreement** specifying remote work terms
2. **Equipment provision** — who provides laptop, monitors, etc.
3. **Internet/utilities reimbursement** — if applicable
4. **Working hours monitoring** — respect right to disconnect
5. **Data security** — VPN requirements, secure access protocols
6. **Tax implications** — employee location may affect tax obligations

### Cross-Border Remote Work
- Georgian employee working remotely for foreign company → Georgian labor law applies
- Foreign employee working remotely from Georgia → Georgian labor law may apply
- **Tax residency**: 183 days+ in Georgia = Georgian tax resident

## 6. Termination of Employment

### Grounds for Termination (Art. 37)
1. **Mutual agreement** — both parties agree
2. **Employee resignation** — 30 days notice (unless contract says otherwise)
3. **Employer termination** — must have valid grounds:
   - Economic (redundancy, reorganization)
   - Disciplinary (gross misconduct, repeated violations)
   - Incompetence (after probation period)
   - Objective impossibility (force majeure)
4. **Fixed-term contract expiry**
5. **Court order**

### Termination Notice Requirements
| Scenario | Notice Period |
|----------|-------------|
| Employee resignation | **30 days** (can be waived) |
| Employer termination | **30 days** minimum |
| During probation | **3 days** |
| Gross misconduct | **Immediate** (with written explanation) |

### Severance Pay
- **1 month salary** minimum if terminated by employer without fault
- May be higher per contract or collective agreement
- Not required for: resignation, probation termination, gross misconduct

## 7. Non-Compete Clauses

### Enforceability in Georgia
Non-compete clauses ARE enforceable under Georgian law but must be **reasonable**:

| Factor | Reasonable Limit |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Duration** | Up to **3 years** maximum (Labor Code); **1-2 years** recommended |
| **Geographic scope** | Must match employer's actual market |
| **Activity scope** | Must be narrow and specific |
| **Compensation** | Should include compensation for the restriction period |

### Legal Requirements
- If non-compete is active **after termination**, employer is **legally required** to compensate the employee during the restriction period
- Non-compete restrictions may remain in force for **no more than 3 years** after termination (Labor Code)

### Best Practices
1. Keep duration **≤ 12 months** (courts more likely to uphold)
2. Define specific **competing activities** (not blanket "IT industry")
3. Provide **monthly compensation** during non-compete period (30-50% of last salary) — this is legally required
4. Include **garden leave** clause as alternative
5. Courts can **modify** unreasonable clauses rather than void them entirely

### NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
- Fully enforceable in Georgia
- No specific duration limit (but must be reasonable)
- Can survive employment termination
- Should specify: scope, duration, exceptions, penalties
- **Trade secret protection** available under unfair competition law

## 8. Foreign Workers in IT

### New IT Residence Permit (September 2025)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Duration | **3 years** |
| Renewals | **3 times** (total 12 years possible) |
| Eligible | IT employees, freelancers, company executives |
| Family | Includes family members |
| Processing | Through Ministry of Internal Affairs |

### Mandatory Work Permits (March 2026)
- All foreign workers need **"right to labor/entrepreneurial activity" permit**
- Apply through Ministry → up to **30 calendar days** processing
- Must obtain permit **before** starting work
- Penalties: **2,000 GEL** for first offense (both worker and employer)
- Doubles for repeat within 1 year, triples thereafter

### Transition Period
- Foreign workers in system before March 1, 2026 → deadline: **January 1, 2027**
- Must obtain proper permits during transition
- Visa-free entry (1 year) for 95+ countries remains for visiting, but NOT working

### Visa-Free Countries (select IT-relevant)
Most EU countries, USA, Canada, UK, Israel, Japan, South Korea, Australia, etc.
Full list at geoconsul.gov.ge

## 9. Contractor vs Employee

### Key Distinctions
| Factor | Employee | Contractor |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Relationship | Subordination | Independence |
| Schedule | Set by employer | Flexible |
| Tools | Provided by employer | Own tools |
| Tax | Employer withholds PIT + pension | Contractor files own taxes |
| IP rights | Work-for-hire may apply | Must be assigned by contract |
| Labor Code | Applies | Does NOT apply |
| Termination | Notice + severance | Per contract terms |

### Risk of Misclassification
- Georgian tax authorities may **reclassify** contractors as employees
- Triggers: fixed schedule, exclusive work, employer tools, same office
- Consequences: back taxes (20% PIT + 4% pension) + penalties
- **Best practice**: Use genuine contractor relationships with clear independence

## 10. Stock Options / Equity Compensation

### Current Legal Framework
- No specific **ESOP/stock option law** in Georgia
- Must be structured through:
  - **Share purchase agreements** under Law on Entrepreneurs
  - **Option contracts** under Civil Code
  - **Phantom equity** or **profit sharing** arrangements

### Tax Treatment
- Stock options at grant → **no tax event**
- Exercise of options → **taxable** as employment income (20% PIT)
- Sale of shares → **5% capital gains** or 20% PIT (depending on structure)
- Dividend income → **5% withholding**

### Practical Implementation
1. Draft option agreement as part of employment contract
2. Use **vesting schedule** (typically 4 years with 1-year cliff)
3. Include **good leaver/bad leaver** provisions
4. Consider **phantom shares** for simplicity (no actual equity transfer)
5. **Get legal advice** — complex area with no standardized framework in Georgia

## 11. Salary Tax Obligations

### Employer Withholding (monthly, by 15th of following month)

| Component | Rate | Base |
|-----------|------|------|
| Income Tax (PIT) | 20% | Gross salary |
| Pension — employee | 2% | Gross salary |
| Pension — employer | 2% | Gross salary (employer cost) |

### Example: 3,000 GEL gross salary

| Item | Amount (GEL) |
|------|-------------|
| Gross salary | 3,000.00 |
| PIT (20%) | -600.00 |
| Pension employee (2%) | -60.00 |
| **Net to employee** | **2,340.00** |
| Pension employer (2%) | 60.00 |
| **Total employer cost** | **3,060.00** |

### Under IC Status (5% PIT)

| Item | Amount (GEL) |
|------|-------------|
| Gross salary | 3,000.00 |
| PIT (5%) | -150.00 |
| Pension employee (2%) | -60.00 |
| **Net to employee** | **2,790.00** |
| Pension employer (2%) | 60.00 |
| **Total employer cost** | **3,060.00** |
