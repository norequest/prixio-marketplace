## Core Tax Rates (2025–2026)

| Tax | Rate | Applies To |
|-----|------|------------|
| Corporate Profit Tax (CIT) | 15% | Distributed profits ONLY (Estonian model) |
| CIT — banks, credit unions, microfinance | 20% | Since 1 Jan 2023 |
| Retained/reinvested profit | 0% | No tax until distributed |
| VAT (დღგ) | 18% | Turnover above 100,000 GEL/12 months |
| VAT on IT exports | 0% | Services to non-Georgian clients |
| Income Tax (სახელფასო) | 20% flat | Employee salaries — employer withholds |
| Pension — employee | 2% | Of gross salary |
| Pension — employer | 2% | Of gross salary (employer pays) |
| Pension — state | 2% | Salary ≤24,000 GEL/yr; 1% for 24k–60k; 0% above 60k |
| Dividend withholding | 5% | Paid to individuals (resident or not) |
| Dividend — from banks/microfinance | 0% | On profits from 2023+ (2026 amendment) |
| Interest income (individuals) | 5% | Withholding at source |
| Royalties (non-resident) | 10% | Withholding at source |
| Payments to tax haven jurisdictions | 15% | Withholding at source |
| Property tax | Up to 1% | Average annual book value of fixed assets |
| Land tax (agricultural) | 5–100 GEL/ha | Adjusted by territorial coefficient (up to 150%) |
| Land tax (non-agricultural) | 0.24 GEL/m² | Adjusted by coefficient (up to 150%) |

## 2026 Tax Code Amendments (Law No. 1061-IVМС-XIМП, effective 1 Jan 2026)

Key changes enacted November 12, 2025:

1. **Special Trading Zones eliminated** — provisions governing special trading zones and market entities removed
2. **Transfer pricing CbC reporting** — new deadline: within 12 months of reporting financial year-end (replaces Dec 31 deadline)
3. **Agricultural tax exemption extended** through Dec 2027 — income from first supply of domestic agricultural goods exempt (threshold: 200,000 GEL annual gross income)
4. **Agricultural cooperative exemptions** extended through 2027 — distributed income from primary turnover and internal settlements exempt
5. **Dividends from banks/microfinance** — not subject to taxation, excluded from aggregate income (for profits from 2023+)
6. **Interest on debt instruments** — preferential treatment continues for Georgian-issued instruments on recognized trading platforms
7. **VAT on real estate/construction** — exemption with full input VAT deduction extended until 2029
8. **VAT on investment gold** — new exemption with full input VAT deduction
9. **Low-value personal imports** — VAT relief maintained until 2028
10. **Profit tax declaration forms updated** — must include info about controlled international transactions (from Jan 2026)
11. **Online gambling license holders** — profit tax increased from 15% to 20%

## Monthly Filing Obligations (15th of every month)

Every LLC in Georgia must file monthly via rs.ge — **even zero declarations**.

### What to file by the 15th:

```
If you have employees:
  ✅ Withholding tax declaration (20% income tax) — Form III-07
  ✅ Pension contribution declaration (2% + 2%) — Form III-09
  ✅ Payment of withheld amounts

If VAT-registered:
  ✅ VAT declaration — Form III-10
  ✅ VAT payment

If profit distributed this month:
  ✅ Corporate profit tax declaration (15%) — Form III-04
  ✅ Dividend withholding (5%)

If purchasing services from abroad (SaaS, cloud, contractors):
  ✅ Reverse-charge VAT declaration (18%) — even for non-VAT-registered businesses
  ✅ VAT-registered businesses can simultaneously claim input VAT (net 0%)

Always:
  ✅ Zero declaration if marked on information card but no activity
```

**Critical**: If you mark a declaration type on your rs.ge information card,
you MUST file it every month — even zeros. Failure = penalty per month.

## VAT Registration

- **Mandatory threshold**: 100,000 GEL taxable turnover in any rolling 12-month period
- **Voluntary**: Can register below threshold (useful for input VAT recovery)
- **IT services exported**: 0% VAT (zero-rated) regardless of registration status
- **Domestic services**: 18% once registered
- **Failure to register at threshold**: 5,000 GEL penalty (Article 274) + retroactive VAT liability
- **Non-resident digital service providers**: Must register for VAT on B2C digital services to Georgian consumers (quarterly reporting by 20th following each quarter)

### Reverse-Charge VAT
Every Georgian business purchasing services from abroad must declare **18% reverse-charge VAT** monthly, even if not VAT-registered. VAT-registered businesses can simultaneously claim input VAT deduction (net 0% effect).

Applies to: SaaS subscriptions, cloud services, foreign contractor payments, any service from non-resident.

### Prixio VAT Logic
- Lotify commissions from Georgian sellers = domestic = 18% once registered
- Courio fees from Georgian senders = domestic = 18% once registered
- If expanding internationally: export portion = 0%
- Foreign SaaS/tools: reverse-charge VAT applies

## SARAS Financial Reporting Categories

Entity classification based on meeting **at least 2 of 3 criteria**:

| Category | Revenue | Assets | Employees | Standard | Audit |
|----------|---------|--------|-----------|----------|-------|
| I (Large) | >100M GEL | >50M GEL | >250 | Full IFRS | Mandatory |
| II (Medium) | >16M GEL | >8M GEL | >50 | IFRS or IFRS SME | Mandatory |
| III (Small) | >2M GEL | >1M GEL | >10 | IFRS for SMEs | Optional |
| IV (Micro) | ≤2M GEL | ≤1M GEL | ≤10 | Simplified (cash-basis OK) | Not required |

**Required documents** (Categories I–III): Balance sheet, income statement, cash flow statement, statement of changes in equity, notes to financial statements, management report.
**Category IV**: Simplified financial statements only.

**Prixio at launch = Category IV** — simplified financial statements, no mandatory audit.
Submission deadline: **October 1** of the following year via **reportal.ge**.
Reports become publicly available on reportal.ge after submission.

## Estonian Profit Tax Model (Key Rules)

```
Revenue earned → stays in company → 0% tax
Revenue distributed as dividends → 15% corporate tax + 5% dividend withholding
Non-business expenses → treated as profit distribution → 15% tax
Personal expenses through company → taxable distribution
Representative expenses exceeding 1% of prior-year revenue → deemed distribution
Interest expenses above 24% annual rate → deemed distribution
Intra-Georgian dividends between legal entities → EXEMPT from CIT
```

**Gross-up calculation**: Tax = Distribution ÷ 0.85 × 0.15 (effective rate 17.647% on net)
**CIT credit mechanism**: When LLC pays 15% CIT on distribution, the 5% dividend withholding is effectively credited, so total burden = 15% (not 15% + 5% = 20%). Net to founder = 80% of gross dividend.

## Special Tax Regimes

### Virtual Zone Person (VZP) Status

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| CIT on qualifying IT exports | **0%** |
| VAT on qualifying IT exports | **0%** |
| Dividend withholding | **5%** (still applies) |
| Eligibility | Certified by GITA (gita.gov.ge) |
| Qualifying activities | Software development, IT services, digital content |
| Key restriction | Only for services delivered to clients OUTSIDE Georgia |
| Does NOT apply to | Lotify/Courio domestic Georgian market revenue |
| Application | Through GITA |
| Retained profits | 0% indefinitely |
| Distributed profits (from exports) | 5% dividend withholding only (no CIT) |

### International Company (IC) Status

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| CIT | **5%** on distributed profits (vs. standard 15%) |
| Dividend tax (non-resident shareholders) | **0%** |
| PIT for employees | **5%** (vs. standard 20%) |
| Property tax | **0%** on property used for qualifying activities (except land) |
| Pension | Standard 2%+2% still applies for Georgian citizens/residents |
| Revenue requirement | ≥98% from permitted activities |
| Operating history | 2+ years in qualifying sectors |
| Qualifying sectors | IT, Maritime |
| Legal structure | Georgian enterprise (or subsidiary with 50%+ parent ownership) |
| Location restriction | Cannot be in free industrial zones |
| Substance requirement | Actual operations in Georgia with qualified personnel |
| Application | Via rs.ge portal; Revenue Service reviews in 10 business days |
| Revocation | If non-qualifying revenue exceeds 2% |
| VAT | No blanket exemption; B2B exports typically exempt |

### GITA Innovative Startup Status (since September 2025)

**Tiered tax benefits over up to 10 years:**

| Period | Salary Income Tax | Corporate Profit Tax |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Years 1–3 | **0%** (exempt up to 10,000 GEL/month per employee) | Standard 15% |
| Years 4–6 | **5%** | **5%** |
| Years 7–10 | **10%** | **10%** |

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Innovation: Create new/improved products/processes with practical value
- Funding: ≥100,000 GEL from registered investment funds/angel networks/accelerators, OR ≥150,000 GEL from GITA
- Revenue must relate to funded innovative activity
- Clean standing: no tax debts, no insolvency
- Legal form: Georgian LLC or JSC
- Registration fee: 200 GEL

**Key restrictions:**
- Cannot simultaneously hold IC Status or VZP Status
- Annual compliance verification required
- Automatically terminates on failure to maintain requirements
- GITA review: 10 calendar days + 5-day remediation window

**Renewal structure:**
- Years 1–2: Initial + two 1-year renewals
- Years 3–5: Need 5M GEL total investment (30% from approved sources)
- Years 6–10: Need 15M GEL total investment by year 6

### Innovative SME Status
- For companies spending ≥5% revenue (min 100,000 GEL) on R&D
- Benefits: Triple R&D deduction for dividend purposes, 20% profit reinvestment grants (max 100,000 GEL)
- Fee: 500 GEL; indefinite duration

### R&D Service Provider Status
- For companies deriving ≥80% revenue from research services
- Benefits: Permanent 5% withholding and 5% CIT
- Fee: 500 GEL; indefinite

### Small Business Status
- **1%** on turnover up to 500,000 GEL/year
- **3%** above 500,000 GEL threshold
- Revoked if threshold exceeded 2 consecutive years

### Micro Business Status
- **0%** tax (individual entrepreneurs only)
- Turnover limit: 30,000 GEL/year

### Free Industrial Zone (FIZ)
- CIT: **0%**
- Dividend tax: **0%**
- Interest withholding: **0%**

| Failure to withhold tax at source | Withholding agent becomes liable + penalties |

## Non-Resident Digital Services VAT

Since recent amendments, non-resident providers of digital/electronic services to Georgian consumers must:
- Register for VAT in Georgia
- Charge 18% on B2C digital services
- Report quarterly by the 20th following each quarter
- Simplified registration available for non-residents

Online marketplaces may be deemed suppliers for VAT purposes.
