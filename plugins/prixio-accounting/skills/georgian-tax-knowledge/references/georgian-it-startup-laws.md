# Georgian Laws Critical for IT Startups

**Research Date:** 2026-03-05
**Source:** matsne.gov.ge (Legislative Herald of Georgia) and official legal analyses

---

## 1. Law on Information Technology Zones

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს კანონი საინფორმაციო ტექნოლოგიების ზონების შესახებ
- **English:** Law of Georgia on Information Technology Zones

### Law Details
- **Number:** Government Ordinance No. 4064-RS
- **Adoption Date:** December 15, 2010 (in force since June 1, 2011)
- **Matsne URL:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/1152405

### Key Articles

**Article 1 - Scope**
The Law defines the legal framework for IT zones and Virtual Zone Persons (VZP) in Georgia.

**Article 2 - Definitions**
- **Virtual Zone Person (VZP / ვირტუალური ზონის პირი):** A legal person engaged in IT activities and holding the appropriate status granted by the government.
- **Information Technologies (IT):** Study, support, development, design, production and introduction of computer information systems, as a result of which software products are obtained.
- **Virtual Zone:** Consists of a Virtual Zone Person or a group of such persons.

**Article 3 - Taxation**
Relationships related to IT activities and taxation of virtual zone persons are regulated by this Law, the Tax Code of Georgia, and other legal acts.

**Article 4 - Regulatory Exemptions**
Virtual zone persons are exempt from any special regulations and do not need licences, permits, or other regulatory documents to conduct their IT activities.

**Article 5 - Granting VZP Status**
The status of a virtual zone person is granted by a person designated by the Government of Georgia, not later than 10 working days after submitting an application. The LEPL Financial-Analytical Service of the Ministry of Finance is the designated authority.

### VZP Status Requirements (per Government Decree)
Reference: https://matsne.gov.ge/ka/document/view/1198594

1. The applicant must be a legal entity registered in Georgia
2. Must be engaged in information technology activities (software development, IT services)
3. Application is submitted electronically to the LEPL Financial-Analytical Service
4. Processing period: 10 working days
5. Certificate is issued digitally and published on the agency website
6. Status is valid indefinitely (unless waived or revoked for tax evasion)
7. Certificate is shared with the Georgian Revenue Service for tax administration

### Practical Implications for IT Startups
- **No licensing required** for IT activities
- **Fully electronic** application and certification process
- **Status is permanent** once granted
- Must maintain local presence with technical staff in Georgia
- Must provide evidence of IT service delivery from Georgian territory
- Revenue must come from services provided to foreign (non-resident) clients for tax exemption

## 2. Tax Code of Georgia (IT-Relevant Provisions)

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს საგადასახადო კოდექსი
- **English:** Tax Code of Georgia

### Law Details
- **Consolidated version on Matsne:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/1043717
- **Original 2010 version:** Adopted September 17, 2010

### Key Articles for IT Startups

#### Article 99(1)(p) - Profit Tax Exemption for VZP
- Profit earned from supplying information technologies created by virtual zone persons **outside Georgia** is **exempt from profit tax (0% CIT)**
- This applies to the distribution of profit (Estonian model - tax is triggered only on distribution)
- **Critical condition:** The exemption does NOT apply to mere electronic provision of services using hardware/software where no software product is created. The activity must involve actual creation of software products.

#### Article 130 - Dividend Withholding Tax for VZP
- Dividends paid by a VZP to natural persons (individuals) are subject to **5% withholding tax** at the source of payment
- The 5% rate may be reduced to 0% under applicable double taxation treaties

#### Article 23 - International Company Status
- **Introduced:** October 8, 2020 (Government Decree No. 619)
- **Reference on Matsne:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/5012362
- Applies to IT and Maritime sector companies
- **Tax Benefits:**
  - Corporate Income Tax (CIT): **5%** (applied only upon profit distribution)
  - Personal Income Tax (PIT) on employee salaries: **5%**
  - Withholding Tax on Dividends: **0%**
  - Property Tax: **0%** (excluding land) on property used for permitted activities
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
  - At least **98% of annual revenue** from permitted IT/Maritime activities
  - Income-generating operations performed **from within Georgia**
  - Meaningful local presence: qualified personnel, operational expenses in Georgia
- **Expense Deductions:** Wages paid to resident Georgian citizens and scientific research/design/testing services in permitted activities can reduce CIT base

### VZP vs International Company Status Comparison

| Feature | VZP | International Company |
|---------|-----|----------------------|
| CIT on export profits | 0% | 5% |
| Dividend tax | 5% | 0% |
| Employee PIT | 20% (standard) | 5% |
| Property tax | Standard | 0% (excl. land) |
| Revenue threshold | No minimum | 98% from IT |
| Best for | Pure software export | Companies with local staff/expenses |

### Filing Requirements for VZP
- Monthly corporate tax declarations with the Revenue Service
- Detailed record-keeping for audits
- Transfer pricing documentation requirements apply (especially for related-party transactions)

## 3. Law on Entrepreneurs

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს კანონი მეწარმეთა შესახებ
- **English:** Law of Georgia on Entrepreneurs

### Law Details
- **New Law Effective:** January 1, 2022 (replacing the 1994 law)
- **Matsne URL:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/5230186
- Derived from EU directives for uniform and transparent corporate framework

### Key Provisions for LLC Formation (შეზღუდული პასუხისმგებლობის საზოგადოება / შპს)

**Minimum Capital:**
- **No minimum share capital** required for LLC registration
- An LLC may form subscribed capital in any amount at its choice
- Contrast: JSC requires minimum GEL 100,000 (25% contributed upon incorporation)

**Registration:**
- Registration through the Entrepreneurial Registry (National Agency of Public Registry - NAPR)
- Extract from Entrepreneurial Registry has same legal force as tax registration certificate (since January 1, 2010)
- Fast registration process (typically 1 business day for standard, same-day expedited available)

**Partners/Shareholders:**
- LLC can have one or more partners
- No restriction on foreign ownership
- Both natural and legal persons can be partners

**Management Structure:**
- Partners appoint director(s) as the executive body
- Articles of association define governance rules
- Flexibility in management structure

**Key Changes in 2022 Law:**
- Stronger minority shareholder protections
- Enhanced corporate governance requirements
- Clearer rules on partner agreements
- 2-year grace period for existing entities to comply with new requirements

### Practical Implications for IT Startups
- Zero capital barrier to entry
- Foreign founders can hold 100% ownership
- Simple and fast registration (1-3 days)
- Flexible management structures suitable for startups
- No restrictions on the number of partners

## 4. Labour Code of Georgia

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს ორგანული კანონი - საქართველოს შრომის კოდექსი
- **English:** Organic Law of Georgia - Labour Code of Georgia

### Law Details
- **Law No.:** 729 (Organic Law)
- **Adoption Date:** June 12, 2013 (with multiple amendments)
- **Matsne URL:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/1155567
- **Recent amendments:** 2025 labour migration law updates

### Key Articles for IT Companies

**Employment Contracts (Articles 12-19):**
- Written contracts mandatory if employment exceeds 1 month
- Verbal contracts allowed for shorter periods (less protection)
- Must specify: position, working conditions, start date, compensation, working hours

**Working Hours (Articles 24-27):**
- Standard working week: **40 hours**
- Overtime: requires employee consent, compensated at increased rate
- Night work (10 PM - 6 AM): compensated at increased rate

**Probation Period (Article 18):**
- Maximum **6 months**
- During probation, either party can terminate with 3 days notice
- Salary during probation cannot be less than minimum wage

**Termination (Articles 37-38):**
- Employer must provide at least **1 month** advance notice (or 3 months for mass layoffs)
- Severance: minimum 1 month salary
- Grounds for termination must be documented

**Non-Compete Clauses:**
- Allowed but must be reasonable in scope and duration
- Must be compensated

### Remote Work Provisions
- **No specific remote work articles** in the Labour Code
- Remote work is not separately regulated by the Labour Code or other Georgian legislation
- However, all labour rules apply equally to remote and in-office employees
- **Employer obligations for remote workers:**
  - Must record employee working time regardless of work location
  - Must reimburse work-related expenses (internet, electricity if applicable)
  - Must maintain occupational safety standards
- Remote work terms should be specified in the employment contract

### Intellectual Property Created by Employees
- Not specifically addressed in the Labour Code
- Governed by the Copyright Law (see Section 6 below)
- Best practice: include IP assignment clauses in employment contracts

### Labour Migration (for foreign tech workers)
- Reference: https://matsne.gov.ge/ka/document/view/2806732
- Foreign nationals can work in Georgia with proper documentation
- Special labour activity permit may be required depending on nationality and duration

### Practical Implications for IT Startups
- Flexible employment framework
- No statutory remote work restrictions (but no specific protections either)
- Must define remote work terms clearly in employment contracts
- Include IP assignment clauses in all developer contracts
- 40-hour work week standard; overtime must be consensual and compensated

## 5. Law on Personal Data Protection

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს კანონი პერსონალურ მონაცემთა დაცვის შესახებ
- **English:** Law of Georgia on Personal Data Protection

### Law Details
- **New Law Adopted:** June 14, 2023
- **In Force:** March 1, 2024
- **Supersedes:** Previous 2011 law
- **Matsne URL (new law):** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/5827307
- **Matsne URL (previous law):** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/1561437
- **GDPR-aligned:** Yes, closely modeled on EU GDPR and Law Enforcement Directive (LED)

### Key Provisions

**Core Principles (aligned with GDPR):**
- Lawfulness, fairness, transparency
- Purpose limitation
- Data minimization
- Accuracy
- Storage limitation
- Integrity and confidentiality
- Accountability

**Scope of Application:**
- Applies to processing of personal data wholly or partly by automated means within Georgia
- Applies to processing by controllers not established in Georgia if using technical means available in Georgia
- Exception: technical means used solely for data transit

**Key Definitions:**
- **Personal Data:** Any information connected to an identified or identifiable natural person
- **Special Categories:** Racial/ethnic origin, political views, religious beliefs, trade union membership, health data, sexual life, biometric and genetic data

**Data Controller and Processor Obligations:**
- Appointment of a **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** (mandatory for certain controllers/processors)
- **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** required for high-risk processing
- **Data breach notification** obligations (to supervisory authority and affected individuals)
- Records of processing activities
- International data transfer restrictions (adequate protection required)

**Data Subject Rights:**
- Right of access
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure
- Right to restriction of processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object

**Supervisory Authority:**
- Personal Data Protection Inspector (now Service) monitors compliance
- Annual reporting to Government and Parliament
- Power to investigate and impose sanctions

### Practical Implications for IT Startups
- Must implement GDPR-level data protection practices
- Appoint DPO if processing personal data at scale
- Conduct DPIAs for new products/services involving personal data
- Implement data breach notification procedures
- If serving EU customers, compliance with Georgian law largely covers GDPR requirements
- International data transfers require adequate safeguards
- Privacy policies and consent mechanisms required

## 6. Law on Copyright and Related Rights

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს კანონი საავტორო და მომიჯნავე უფლებების შესახებ
- **English:** Law of Georgia on Copyright and Related Rights

### Law Details
- **Law No.:** 2388
- **Original Adoption:** June 22, 1999 (replacing Articles 488-528 of the 1964 Civil Code)
- **Multiple amendments** (most recently 2024)
- **Matsne URL:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/16198
- **Influenced by:** EU copyright directives

### Key Articles for Software/IT

**Article 5 - Protected Works:**
- Copyright applies to scientific, literary and artistic works that are the result of intellectual-creative activities
- Protection arises automatically upon creation (no registration required)

**Article 6 - Types of Protected Works:**
- Paragraph 1 explicitly lists **computer programs** as literary works
- Software code is protected as a literary work under Georgian copyright law
- This includes both source code and object code

**Article 16 - Works for Hire (Service Works):**
- The original holder of copyright is the author (the person who created the work)
- **Exception for works for hire:** When a work is created under an employment contract (service work), the economic rights may belong to the employer
- The specific terms should be defined in the employment contract
- **Important:** Moral rights (attribution, integrity) always remain with the author

**Database Protection:**
- The law also covers database producer rights
- Protects the investment in obtaining, verifying, or presenting database contents

**Temporary Copies:**
- Temporary copies that are incidental/necessary to technical processes are permitted
- Important for cloud computing and SaaS operations

### Registration (Optional)
- Copyright protection is automatic upon creation
- Optional registration available through SAKPATENTI (National Intellectual Property Center of Georgia)
- Reference: https://www.sakpatenti.gov.ge

### Practical Implications for IT Startups
- Software is automatically protected by copyright upon creation
- **Must include IP assignment clauses in employment contracts** to ensure company owns code written by employees
- Moral rights cannot be transferred (attribution right stays with developer)
- Database rights protect investments in data compilation
- Consider registering key software with SAKPATENTI for evidentiary purposes
- Open-source licensing is compatible with Georgian copyright law

## 7. Law on Information Security

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს კანონი ინფორმაციული უსაფრთხოების შესახებ
- **English:** Law of Georgia on Information Security

### Law Details
- **Adoption Date:** June 5, 2012
- **In Force:** July 1, 2012
- **Matsne URL:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/1679424
- **PDF:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/download/1679424/3/en/pdf

### Key Provisions

**Scope:**
- Applies to all legal persons and state authorities that are **critical information system subjects**
- Also applies to organizations related to critical system subjects through labour, contractual, or other relationships

**Critical Information System Subjects:**
- List approved by Government Ordinance (submitted by Ministry of Justice, agreed with Ministries of Defence, Internal Affairs, and State Security Service)
- Includes: state agencies, banks, telecommunications companies, energy infrastructure, transport systems
- **Most private IT startups are NOT classified as critical information system subjects** unless they provide services to government or critical infrastructure

**CERT.GOV.GE:**
- Computer Emergency Response Team of the Data Exchange Agency (now Digital Governance Agency)
- Manages incidents against information security in Georgia's cyberspace
- May request access to information assets during incident response

**Requirements for Critical System Subjects:**
- Implement **ISO 27001** standards
- Conduct regular security audits
- Appoint information security managers
- Report to Digital Governance Agency or Operational-Technical Agency
- Inventory and assessment of all information assets
- Classification of assets by importance, value, security level
- Incident reporting within strict timelines

### Practical Implications for IT Startups
- **Most IT startups are not directly subject** to this law unless providing services to critical infrastructure or government
- If contracting with government agencies, must comply with information security requirements
- ISO 27001 certification may be required for government contracts
- Good practice to follow the law's standards even if not legally required
- Incident reporting obligations if serving critical system subjects

## 8. Law on Electronic Commerce

### Official Name
- **Georgian:** საქართველოს კანონი ელექტრონული კომერციის შესახებ
- **English:** Law of Georgia on Electronic Commerce

### Law Details
- **Adoption Date:** June 2023
- **Fully Implemented:** Since 2024
- **PDF:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/download/5825902/0/en/pdf
- **Part of:** EU Association Agreement compliance measures

### Key Provisions

**No Licensing Requirement (Article on Freedom of Services):**
- Provision of commercial services over the internet is **not subject to licensing, permits, or authorization**
- Exception: activities already subject to licence/permit regimes under other Georgian laws

**Service Provider Information Obligations:**
- Must ensure permanent and easy accessibility of:
  - Company identification number (from Entrepreneurial Registry)
  - Contact information (email, phone, fax)
  - Clear and detailed pricing (including delivery fees, taxes)
  - Terms and conditions of service

**Consumer Protection:**
- Service fees must be determined clearly and comprehensibly
- All expenses including delivery and taxes must be disclosed
- Right of withdrawal for consumers (cooling-off period)
- Service provider responsible for accuracy of provided information

**Intermediary Service Provider Liability:**
- **No general monitoring obligation** - intermediary service providers are not required to monitor information conveyed or stored
- No obligation to actively detect illegal activities
- Safe harbor provisions for hosting and caching services

**Enforcement:**
- National Competition Agency of Georgia oversees compliance
- Maximum penalty: **2% of previous year's annual turnover**
- Annual reporting by the Agency on e-commerce activities (published by May 1)

### Related Law: Electronic Documents and Electronic Trust Services
- **Matsne URL:** https://matsne.gov.ge/en/document/view/3654557
- Governs electronic signatures, electronic seals, timestamps
- Provides legal validity to electronic contracts and documents

### Practical Implications for IT Startups
- No special licensing needed for e-commerce/SaaS activities
- Must provide transparent company and pricing information on websites/apps
- Consumer-facing services require clear terms and withdrawal rights
- Platform businesses benefit from intermediary liability safe harbors
- Electronic contracts are legally valid and enforceable
- Compliance with EU-aligned standards facilitates cross-border commerce

## Quick Reference: Tax Regimes for IT Startups in Georgia

### Option 1: Virtual Zone Person (VZP)
- **CIT:** 0% on IT export profits
- **Dividends:** 5% withholding
- **Employee PIT:** 20% (standard)
- **VAT:** 0% on exports
- **Best for:** Software companies exporting products/services with minimal local expenses

### Option 2: International Company Status
- **CIT:** 5% on distributed profits
- **Dividends:** 0%
- **Employee PIT:** 5%
- **Property Tax:** 0% (excl. land)
- **Best for:** IT companies with significant local workforce and expenses

### Option 3: Standard Georgian LLC
- **CIT:** 15% (Estonian model - on distribution only)
- **Dividends:** 5%
- **Employee PIT:** 20%
- **VAT:** 18% (threshold: GEL 100,000 annual turnover)
- **Small Business Status:** 1% of gross revenue (if turnover < GEL 500,000)
